Minimum Viable Product

A Minimum Viable Product (MVP) is a version of a product with just enough features to satisfy early customers and provide feedback for future product development. It's a strategy used to quickly bring a new product to market, allowing for real-world testing and validation while minimizing the resources and risks associated with fully building out the product.

The session on Lean Startup and MVP, held on August 9, 2024, featured speakers Dr. Nadir Charniya, Mr. Amit Singh, and Mr. Amit Rambhia. Dr. Charniya began by emphasizing the importance of problem-solving in both work and life, using Amitabh Bachchan as an example of how engaging with others can lead to better solutions. He then introduced the concept of a startup as a business focused on innovation, discussing the Lean Startup method, which involves rapidly developing products with limited resources while ensuring they meet market needs. He illustrated this with practical examples, such as a bus crowd management system and a garbage-cleaning robot.

Following this, Mr. Amit Rambhia shared his entrepreneurial journey, highlighting the significance of identifying market problems and seizing opportunities where others see obstacles. He encouraged participants to consider starting their own businesses, offering practical advice and emphasizing confidence and determination.
